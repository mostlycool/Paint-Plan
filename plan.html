Living Room Accent Wall Refinishing

Objective: Refresh vaulted accent wall — exact color match, professional finish, budget-conscious

Project Proposal — Executive Summary

Estimated time: 2–3 days • Supplies: $120–$200

Goal: Repaint the main vaulted accent wall, matching existing color with spectrometer color match. Prioritize long-lasting, washable finish (eggshell) and a crisp professional look. Work scope below.

Scope of Work

  • Accurate area measurement and material calculations
  • Physical paint chip — spectrometer color match
  • Full surface prep: clean, repair, sand
  • One coat latex primer + two coats premium eggshell paint
  • Professional cleanup and room reset

Why this approach?

  • Primer ensures uniform absorption and color fidelity
  • Two finish coats for durability and deep color
  • Eggshell/satin avoids shine while remaining washable
  • One gallon of paint/primer is sufficient for this 200 sq ft wall

Detailed DIY Guide — Step-by-Step

Follow this checklist during the job

Phase 1 — Measurement & Color Match

Wall area calculation (rectangle + triangle) = 200 sq ft. One gallon of primer and one gallon of paint are sufficient for primer + two finish coats for this surface.

  1. Cut 1" paint chip behind an outlet or switch with a utility knife.
  2. Bring chip to Sherwin-Williams / Home Depot / Lowe's for spectrometer match.

Phase 2 — Shopping & Tools

Buy quality mid-range primer and paint (eggshell). See the shopping summary on the right for itemized prices.

Phase 3 — Prep & Paint (detailed steps)

Cleaning: TSP substitute or degreasing cleaner, wipe and let dry
Repair: scrape bubbling, apply spackling in thin layers, let dry between coats
Sand spackling flush, wipe dust with damp cloth
Prime: cut in edges with angled brush, roll with 9" roller in W patterns
Paint: two finish coats; cut in, then roll; maintain wet edge
Remove tape at 45° while paint is slightly tacky for crisp lines
Cleanup: wash brushes/rollers, reseal paint can, reattach plates after 24 hours

Phase 4 — Final Steps

  • Label leftover paint with date & room (keeps matching easy)
  • Store brush/rolls if reusing; discard if cheap single-use

Pro Tips & Safety

  • Always test the matched paint on a small area — view in daylight and incandescent lighting.
  • Work in well-ventilated conditions if using stronger cleaners.
  • Wear a dust mask when sanding spackle.
  • If the wall has significant bubbling across a large area, consider consulting a pro for drywall replacement.
